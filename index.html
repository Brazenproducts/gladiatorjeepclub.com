How to Choose the Right Jeep Gladiator Accessories

The Jeep Gladiator JT is a vehicle that rewards thoughtful upgrades. Unlike a basic commuter car where accessories are purely cosmetic, every bolt-on you add to a JT either improves your trail capability, your daily comfort, or your vehicle's resale value. The problem is the aftermarket for the Gladiator is enormous — and a lot of it is generic junk that doesn't fit right, degrades quickly, or simply doesn't work the way it's advertised.

The good news: the JT community is tight-knit and vocal. The forums, Reddit threads (r/JeepGladiator), and Facebook groups surface the real winners fast. What we've done is distill that community wisdom alongside our own research to give you a shortlist that actually delivers.

Here's the framework we use when evaluating any Gladiator accessory:

  • JT-specific fitment — Does it say "JT" or "Gladiator" in the compatibility list? JL/JLU items sometimes fit but not always. Never assume.
  • Build quality and materials — Look for mil-spec nylon, 3M-backed adhesives, aircraft aluminum, or marine-grade hardware. Cheap ABS plastic and thin stamped steel don't survive trail use.
  • Verified reviews from JT owners — 4.5 stars or higher with 50+ reviews, specifically from Gladiator owners — not just Wrangler users who may have different fit experiences.
  • Reversibility — The best mods are ones you can undo. If you ever want to sell your JT or return to stock, you want options that uninstall cleanly.
  • Made in USA when possible — For safety-adjacent items (seat covers, grab handles, tow gear), domestic manufacturing is worth paying for. Bartact is the gold standard example.

Top Performance Upgrades for the Jeep Gladiator JT (Without Breaking the Bank)

Once the interior is sorted, most Gladiator owners turn their attention to performance. The JT's 3.6L Pentastar V6 — or the 3.0L EcoDiesel if you went that route — is a capable engine that benefits from a few targeted upgrades without needing a full build or a dyno tune.

The Pedal Commander is the easiest and most impactful starting point. The factory throttle calibration is deliberately conservative to meet emissions standards and deliver acceptable fuel economy on the EPA test cycle. The Pedal Commander doesn't change horsepower — it changes how the existing power is delivered. Most JT owners set it to City mode at +20 for daily driving and switch to a lower sensitivity setting when crawling technical obstacles where precise throttle modulation matters more than instant response. Installation takes five minutes and it's completely reversible — unplug it and you're back to stock with no trace.

Beyond throttle response, here's the performance upgrade ladder most JT owners climb in order:

  1. Cold air intake — Improves throttle response and adds a modest power bump across the RPM range. S&B and K&N both make JT-specific kits with lifetime warranties.
  2. Axle-back or cat-back exhaust — The JT's factory exhaust is quiet to a fault. An axle-back gives it the deep V6 note it should have shipped with without requiring any tuning.
  3. 2.5"–3.5" lift kit — The sweet spot for tire clearance without requiring major geometry correction. Rough Country, Teraflex, and Fox all have JT-specific kits that work with the Gladiator's unique wheelbase.
  4. 35" tires — The natural progression after a modest lift. Plan for a regear (4.56 or 4.88 ratio) if you're going 35" or larger to restore normal acceleration feel and protect the transmission.
  5. Regear — Often overlooked until owners feel the sluggishness of stock 3.73s with 35" rubber. Non-negotiable for serious off-roaders; a good shop can do both axles in a day.

The trail awning is the most transformative bed accessory for anyone who uses the JT for camping or overlanding. When you're out for a weekend, that truck bed becomes your kitchen, gear storage, and sleeping platform. An awning that clips to the bed rail or topper side creates an outdoor shade structure in seconds — extending your usable space significantly in both hot and rainy conditions. The Sport Trail Topper awning in our picks is sized for the Gladiator bed (57x64") and weighs only 5 lbs, so it lives in the bed permanently without eating meaningfully into payload or mpg.

Other bed upgrades worth the investment:

  • Tonneau cover — Keeps bed contents dry and reduces aerodynamic drag. Fold-up soft covers work best for JT owners who alternate trail use with hauling lumber or gear.
  • Bed rack — Elevates storage to the top of the bed, creating space underneath for more gear. Essential for full overlanding setups with rooftop tents or gear chests.
  • Tailgate table — A lightweight aluminum table that mounts in the tailgate receiver for camp cooking, trail-side repairs, or tailgating. Folds flat when not in use.
  • Aftermarket tie-down cleats — The JT's factory tie-down anchors are minimal. Upgraded rail anchors or bed cleats give you far more flexibility for securing gear, bikes, or cargo.

The JT bed truly rewards creativity. Does the interior trim kit fit both the JL Wrangler and JT Gladiator?
Yes — the dashboard, HVAC, center console, and door pull trims share the same form factor across the JL, JLU, and JT. Always confirm in the product listing, but both kits in our top picks explicitly list Gladiator JT compatibility.
What's the difference between the Jeep Gladiator JT and the Wrangler JL?
The Gladiator JT is a 5-foot pickup truck version of the JL Wrangler. It shares the same front end, engine options, 4x4 system, and interior layout forward of the B-pillar, but adds a full pickup truck bed in the rear. Most JL interior accessories fit the JT; body and bed accessories are JT-specific.
What's the best first upgrade for a new Jeep Gladiator owner?
Seat covers, without question. The Gladiator is a tool, not a museum piece — protecting the factory upholstery from day one preserves resale value and makes daily life easier. After that: throttle response (Pedal Commander), interior trim kit, and a proper metal phone mount.
Does the Pedal Commander void the Jeep Gladiator warranty?
No — the Pedal Commander is a plug-in device that works with the factory ECU. It doesn't alter engine calibration or write to any modules. Under the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, dealers cannot void your warranty simply for using a plug-in throttle controller. Always unplug it before dealer service visits to keep things simple.
